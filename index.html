What is Leiomyosarcoma?

Leiomyosarcoma (LMS) is a rare and aggressive type of soft tissue sarcoma that arises from smooth muscle cells, typically found in the uterus, stomach, intestines, blood vessels, and other organs. It accounts for approximately 10-20% of all soft tissue sarcomas and is diagnosed in only about 4 out of every 1 million people per year. This cancer is often resistant to chemotherapy and can spread to other parts of the body, making early detection and specialized treatment crucial. Jennifer Jane Pope was diagnosed with this challenging disease, and her fight against LMS showcases her incredible strength and resilience.
